Videography

Routine

Similar to "disrupting the loop", this video commentates on how students are constantly living in a loop as we follow the same routine every day. We live the same day, every day. At the same time, it explores the concept of change; the transition phase of going to college and the challenges that one may face in a new environment. The video aspect shows a mundane day in the life of a student following her everyday routine. In contrast, the narration provides a more exciting, vulnerable confession of her feelings.

I want you more than any blue sky.

Weathering With You is a movie about a sunshine girl who has the ability to control weather and fulfill people’s weather wishes in gloomy Tokyo. In turn, she disappears from the world. It’s a love story where a boy falls in love with the sunshine girl, and would rather the weather remain gloomy forever than to lose the girl he loves. For myself, rainy days remind me of the lyrics “let the rain wash away all the pain of yesterday…”. This picture movie aims to convey the feelings of love and pain.

Dear Custodians, Thank You

This documentary short film follows the day in the work life of custodian Maria. It highlights the effort and carefulness school custodians put into maintaining a clean and comfortable living space for students, yet it also gives attention to how students ignore custodians and their work as they simply walk past without saying "hello" or "thank you".

disrupting the loop

As humans, we get sucked into routines and life becomes a loop of the same events happening almost every single day. As a student, my typical day goes as follows: wake up, eat breakfast, go to school, to class, eat lunch, go back to class, go home, shower, eat dinner, do homework, go to bed. While being productive is not something bad, following a routine where breaks are not present can become a concern. This video aims to point out our routined lifestyles and suggest that it is necessary to do something different, to take time off, to relax, and to give ourselves a break after doing the same things over and over again every single day.

* The prompt of this video was to use a selection of pre-recorded clips provided by my peers while still creating our own stories.

Home Cooked Meals

I grew up being spoiled by my mother’s cooking. As a child, I often sat around waiting to eat or help set up tables. I was never truly “in the kitchen” or paid attention to how much time it requires to make a dish, or how carefully our meals are being prepared for us. The slow pace of this video showcases and forces us to recognize how much effort the chef puts into making sure that the fish is evenly cooked, carefully moving the wok to spread the oil, and patiently waiting as the fish is ready to be flipped.
